Ian T. Adams' research agenda focuses on the intersection of policing and policy evaluation, with a specific interest in body-worn cameras and front-line policing. He has over twenty peer-reviewed publications on these and related topics, and his work has been published in top general interest journals of both criminal justice and public administration. His interests also include criminal justice policy, human capital in policing, survey methodology, and he has a Ph.D. in Political Science from the University of Utah.
